ABOUT TOXIC TIE

We're Sun, Sonia, Bryanna, and Sean: four students from UC Berkeley currently taking Critical Making (NM203/ME205). Critical Making is a studio-based class in which students create "future computational experiences that critically explore social and culturally relevant technological themes". For our final project, the four of us wanted to focus our design around the issue of misogyny in the workplace.

Our website allows women to input misogynstic phrases that they have heard in the workplace to help train our tie to better react in these situations. Users can also view a bulletin of all of the crowdsourced phrases our tie currently recognizes.

We’re trying to make a statement on misogyny in the workplace. We’re creating a tie that can recognize when misogynistic words or phrases are spoken and flicks up and taps the wearer on the face to let them know that they need to check themselves. It’s a way to take action on this kind of sexism without having to worry about people facing consequences for standing up and calling someone out.
